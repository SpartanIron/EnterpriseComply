INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
Version: 1.0 | Review Cycle: Annual | Classification: Internal

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This Information Security Policy establishes the overarching framework for protecting [Organization Name]'s information assets, systems, and data. It applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and third-party service providers who access organizational systems or data.

This policy is the master policy for the information security program. All subordinate security policies (Access Control, Incident Response, Vulnerability Management, etc.) derive their authority from this document.

2. POLICY STATEMENT
[Organization Name] is committed to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets entrusted to us by customers, partners, and employees. Security is a business function, not a technical afterthought. Every employee has a role in maintaining the security of organizational systems and data.

3. SECURITY PROGRAM AUTHORITY
3.1 The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) or designated Security Lead is responsible for:
- Developing, implementing, and maintaining the information security program
- Reporting security posture and incidents to executive leadership
- Ensuring compliance with applicable laws, regulations, and contractual obligations
- Overseeing all subordinate security policies and procedures

3.2 The security program reports directly to [CEO / CTO / Board] to ensure appropriate authority and independence.

4. INFORMATION SECURITY OBJECTIVES
The security program is designed to achieve the following objectives:
- Protect customer and organizational data from unauthorized access, disclosure, or destruction
- Maintain availability of critical systems and services per documented SLA commitments
- Comply with applicable frameworks: SOC 2 Type II, CMMC Level 2, and FedRAMP Moderate
- Detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents within defined SLAs
- Continuously improve security posture through risk management and monitoring

5. RISK MANAGEMENT
5.1 [Organization Name] conducts formal risk assessments at least annually and following any significant change to the environment, threat landscape, or organizational structure.
5.2 Risk assessments use a likelihood-impact scoring methodology. All Critical and High risks must have documented remediation plans with target dates.
5.3 Risks that cannot be immediately remediated must be formally accepted by the CISO and documented in the risk register.

6. SECURITY ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
- Executive Leadership: Provide resources and authority for the security program; accept organizational risk
- CISO / Security Lead: Own the security program; maintain this policy; report to leadership
- All Employees: Follow security policies; complete required training; report incidents
- IT/Engineering: Implement technical controls; maintain systems within policy; respond to incidents
- Third-party Vendors: Comply with applicable security requirements per contractual agreements

7. MANDATORY SECURITY CONTROLS
All organizational systems and personnel must comply with the following baseline controls:

7.1 Access Control
- All accounts require unique user IDs; shared accounts are prohibited
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all accounts accessing production systems
- Access is granted on a least-privilege basis
- Access reviews are conducted quarterly for privileged accounts and annually for all other accounts

7.2 Encryption
- All data in transit must use TLS 1.2 or higher
- All data at rest must use AES-256 or equivalent
- Encryption keys must be managed per the Key Management Procedure

7.3 Logging and Monitoring
- Security-relevant events must be logged and retained for a minimum of three (3) years
- Logs must be protected from unauthorized modification
- Security events must be reviewed by the security team on a defined schedule

7.4 Incident Response
- All suspected security incidents must be reported immediately to security@[organization].com
- The Incident Response Plan governs detection, containment, eradication, recovery, and lessons learned
- Critical incidents must be contained within 4 hours of detection

7.5 Vulnerability Management
- Production systems must be scanned for vulnerabilities at least monthly
- Critical CVEs must be patched within 15 days; High within 30 days; Medium within 90 days
- All vulnerability findings must be tracked to closure in the risk register

8. POLICY COMPLIANCE
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or contract. Suspected violations should be reported to the CISO. Violations of applicable law will be reported to appropriate authorities.

9. EXCEPTIONS
Exceptions to this policy require written approval from the CISO. All exceptions must be documented, time-limited, and include compensating controls. Exceptions are reviewed at least annually.
