CRITICAL GAPS (Tier 1 — Block sales without these)
1. Integration Catalog Depth

Vanta: 250+ integrations. Drata: 100+. Secureframe: 150+.
ColorComply: 1 live (GitHub). 8 listed as "coming soon."
The #1 complaint across every competitor on G2 is shallow integrations. A prospect's first question is always "do you connect to AWS/Okta/Google Workspace?" If the answer is no, they leave.
Need: AWS, Google Workspace, Okta, Azure AD, Slack as priority 2-5. Each adds 4-8 automated control tests.
2. Continuous Monitoring / Drift Alerts

All Tier 1 competitors run background checks every 24-72 hours and alert on drift.
ColorComply: no real-time monitoring, no alerting. Controls are point-in-time only.
67% of production incidents trace back to config drift. Buyers need proof of continuous compliance, not a snapshot.
Need: Background job runner, webhook listeners, per-integration polling, Slack/email notification system.
3. Risk Register

Required for ISO 27001 (clause 6.1), SOC 2 CC3, and virtually every other framework.
ColorComply: missing entirely. No risk table, no risk owners, no likelihood/impact scoring, no treatment tracking.
Vanta, Drata, Hyperproof all have full risk registers.
Need: Risk table (asset, threat, likelihood 1-5, impact 1-5, inherent score, treatment, residual score, owner, due date).
4. Auditor Collaboration Portal

Auditors need a controlled workspace: request evidence, mark items accepted/rejected, leave comments, download packages.
ColorComply: no auditor workflow. Evidence vault exists but there's no way to share it with an external auditor.
Vanta and Drata both have dedicated auditor-facing portals. This is a deal-blocker at the point of actual audit.
Need: Auditor invite flow, read-only scoped access, evidence request threads, audit package export.
HIGH-VALUE GAPS (Tier 2 — Lose deals to Vanta/Drata without these)
5. Trust Center (Public Security Page)

A public-facing, branded portal where companies share their compliance posture with customers and prospects. Replaces the "fill out this 80-question SIG questionnaire" email chain.
Vanta's Trust Center is one of its top selling features. Buyers pay for Vanta specifically to get the Trust Center URL they can share.
ColorComply: not built.
Need: Public /trust page per org showing active frameworks, passing %, certifications, redacted controls, NDA-gated document requests.
6. Security Questionnaire Automation (SQA)

Companies receive an average of 23% more vendor security questionnaires per quarter. Manual completion takes 3 weeks and 12-18 expert hours per request.
All Tier 1 players offer AI-assisted questionnaire response using past answers and existing controls.
ColorComply: not built.
Need: Questionnaire intake (paste/upload SIG, VSA, custom), AI-match answers to existing controls and evidence, export completed questionnaire.
7. SPRS Score Calculator

For CMMC L2, every DoD contractor must have a valid SPRS (Supplier Performance Risk System) score. Average score is -12 (against a target of +110). Only 1% of the DIB feels prepared.
No competitor has a clean SPRS scoring tool. This is a federal-layer gap that is ColorComply's home turf.
ColorComply: CMMC L2 framework exists but no SPRS scoring.
Need: SPRS calculator using the DoD's exact 110-control weighted scoring (-203 to +110), linked to UCO control results, downloadable SPRS assessment report.
8. SSP (System Security Plan) Generator

FedRAMP and CMMC both mandate a formal SSP. It's 80-300 pages of structured documentation.
No tool generates a well-structured SSP. This is universally painful.
ColorComply: not built.
Need: SSP template wizard (system description, boundary diagram, control implementation statements per UCO control), export to DOCX/PDF.
9. AI Policy Gap Analysis + Generation

The market is moving fast here. Drata announced agentic compliance in Q2 2025. Secureframe added AI RMF and ISO 42001.
Buyers are asking "can your AI generate a draft Password Policy for me?" and "what controls am I missing to be FedRAMP-ready?"
ColorComply: no AI features at all.
Need: AI gap analysis ("you're 74% FedRAMP ready — here are the 11 failing controls"), AI policy draft generation from templates using org context, AI control description generation.
COMPETITIVE PARITY GAPS (Tier 3 — Table stakes, differentiated by quality)
10. Outbound Vendor Risk Assessments

ColorComply has a vendor list but can't send questionnaires TO vendors. This is a core part of third-party risk management (required in SOC 2 CC9.2, ISO 27001 A.15).
Need: Assessment templates (SIG-Lite, CAIQ), send/track/score vendor responses.
11. Access Review Workflows

Structured, manager-approval access reviews for SOC 2 CC6.2 and ISO 27001 A.9.
People page exists but there's no review workflow (approve/revoke, attestation record).
Need: Scheduled access review campaigns, manager email approval links, export attestation records as evidence.
12. Custom Framework Builder

Large customers always have internal control frameworks or niche frameworks (NERC CIP, HITRUST, FedRAMP+ custom overlays) not in the catalog.
All Tier 1 competitors allow custom frameworks. ColorComply has a hard-coded 12.
Need: Custom framework editor: import control list, map to UCO, track separately.
13. Notification System

No Slack/email alerts for: control drift, evidence expiring, new findings, POA&M items overdue.
This is table stakes for "continuous compliance."
Need: Notification rules engine + Slack webhook + email digest.
14. Audit Trail / Change Log

SOC 2 and FedRAMP both require evidence of who changed what, when. Auditors ask for this.
ColorComply: no change log surfaced in the UI.
Need: Per-org audit log table, surfaced in Settings.
The Federal Moat: ColorComply's Biggest Untapped Opportunity
The Tier 1 tools (Vanta, Drata, Secureframe) are fundamentally commercial-sector tools that bolt on a FedRAMP checklist. None of them are purpose-built for the federal market. This creates a clear wedge:

FedRAMP 20x (announced March 2025): The program is shifting to continuous validation, machine-readable evidence, and automated compliance pipelines instead of point-in-time audits. The PMO has explicitly said 80%+ of requirements should have automated validation. No vendor has built for this yet. ColorComply, with its UCO engine and federal-first design, is better positioned to own this than anyone.

Specific federal-layer features that would be 6-18 months ahead of Vanta/Drata:

SPRS scoring (critical now, CMMC Phase 2 mandatory from 2026)
SSP auto-generation with machine-readable OSCAL format output (FedRAMP 20x requirement)
ATO package builder (System Security Plan + SAP + SAR + CIS package)
ConMon dashboard (FedRAMP continuous monitoring: monthly vulnerability scan uploads, POA&M aging, deviation requests)
OSCAL import/export (the machine-readable standard FedRAMP 20x is mandating)
